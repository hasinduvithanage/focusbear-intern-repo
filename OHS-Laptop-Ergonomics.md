What are the risks of using a laptop without an external monitor or keyboard?

Since the screen and keyboard are joined, either the user's hands will be at the wrong position or the screen height will be at the wrong position. 

What ergonomic equipment can improve posture when working on a laptop? (e.g., laptop stands, external keyboards, ergonomic chairs)

Can use laptop stands to increase the screen to eye level. 
Can use an external mouse and keyboard to allow elbows to be at 90 degrees. 
Can use an ergonomic chair to provide support to the vertebrate.

What adjustments should be made to monitor height, chair position, and desk setup for a healthier workspace?

The monitor should be positioned so that the top third of the screen is at eye level.
Chair should be in a way so that my feet are flat on the ground.
The desk should be in a way where my elbows should form a 90 degree angle.

What are some daily habits that reduce the impact of prolonged laptop use? (e.g., movement breaks, posture checks, eye strain reduction techniques)

Take breaks every 20 minutes while using the laptop to look at something far away for about 20 seconds to reduce strain on eyes.
After every hour, stand up and stretch for 2 minutes.
Make sure that my posture is correct once in a while, so that there will be reduced strain on my back.
