What are the main Agile ceremonies, and what is their purpose?
Daily Stand-ups
This is a short meeting that makes sure that everyone on the team is on track and doing progress. These helps identify problems and can be used to discuss how to get rid of them.

Sprint Planning (Scrum) vs. Continuous Prioritization (Kanban)
Sprint planning is done at the start of a sprint. The team will identify a number of tasks and will focus on finishing them by the end of the sprint. Whereas Kanban is continous and fluid. The developers will continously work on the next prioritzed item from the top of the column whenever they become free.

Retrospectives
This is where the team's process is investigated and checking how it can be improved. The outcome of this meeting is an action plan to improve the processes of the team.

Backlog Refinement
This is where the team reviews, updates and prioritizes the action items in the backlog. This is to make sure that they are actionable, clearly defined, and accurately estimated to be carried out.

How do Agile teams collaborate asynchronously and across time zones?
By using strict documentations as soon as a team member clocks out, the team members in a different time zone can pick up the work right where it is left off. By having detailed task descriptions and by setting clear goals, all of the team members will know what to do without needing a live meeting. With pull requests by not just listing the changes but explaining why the change was made, we can avoid a back and forth and get to the point.
